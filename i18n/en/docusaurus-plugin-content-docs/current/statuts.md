# NumaHop Association Bylaws

## PREAMBLE

Open-source professional software is relatively rare and requires close collaboration between developers and users to remain operational and faithful to the principles of sharing and distributing free software. The NumaHOP Association provides a space for exchange, training, and collaborative work around this professional software.

## ARTICLE 1 – NAME

An association is hereby founded among the members agreeing to these bylaws, governed by the law of July 1, 1901 and the decree of August 16, 1901, under the name: Association NumaHOP.

## ARTICLE 2 – PURPOSE

The purpose of this association is the maintenance, development, documentation, promotion, and distribution of the free software NumaHOP.

It aims in particular to:
- encourage collaboration and mutual support among NumaHOP users through the sharing of information, documentation, and resources;
- undertake any type of outreach and training action to promote the use of NumaHOP;
- establish itself as a recognized contact for public or private stakeholders interested in NumaHOP;
- support and participate in development requests for the NumaHOP software.

To this end, the association in particular:
- organizes meetings, study days, workshops, training sessions, etc.;
- forms thematic working groups;
- pools resources, experiences, and expertise;
- participates in the monitoring or funding of NumaHOP developments;
- and any other action that meets its stated objectives.

## ARTICLE 3 – REGISTERED OFFICE

The registered office is located at:

_Bibliothèque de Sorbonne Université, Tour Zamansky, 15th floor – PO Box 192 – 4 place Jussieu, 75252 PARIS CEDEX 05_

It may be transferred by simple decision of the board.

## ARTICLE 4 – DURATION

The duration of the association is unlimited.

## ARTICLE 5 – MEMBERSHIP

The association is composed of natural or legal persons, honorary members, benefactor members, and active members, hereinafter referred to as members, who are current in their membership fees. To become a member, an application must be submitted to the board of the association. Membership is validated by the board. The board may refuse one or more applicants without being required to provide reasons.

Membership in the association implies a commitment to participate in its operation and collective management.

## ARTICLE 6 – BOARD

The board is responsible for the day-to-day management of the association and the implementation of decisions made by the general assembly.

The association is administered by a board of at least 3 and up to 7 members, elected by the general assembly for two years, and eligible for re-election. The board consists of a president, a treasurer, and a secretary. Where applicable, a vice-president, assistant secretary, assistant treasurer, and work coordinator may be designated. In the event of a vacancy, the board provisionally fills the position of the absent delegate. A permanent replacement is made at the next general assembly.

## ARTICLE 7 – BOARD OPERATIONS

Board members must maintain close contact to best administer the association, including by electronic means. Decisions are made by a majority vote of board members. In the event of a tie, the president's vote counts double.

Board meetings are convened by email by the president, assisted by the secretary. The board can only validly deliberate if more than half of its members are present. Meetings are chaired by the president or, by proxy, by the secretary if the president or vice-president cannot attend.

To assist in its duties, the board may appoint project managers for specific tasks: communications, working group facilitation, etc.

## ARTICLE 8 – RESOURCES

The association's resources come from:

- membership fees paid by members, which must be paid annually. The amount is set by the ordinary general assembly;
- grants from the State, local authorities, or any other public or private body;
- individual donations or donations from public interest organizations.

A bank or postal account in the name of the association is held at an institution designated by the board. The general assembly designates the authorized signatories. The association's accounting is maintained in accordance with applicable legislation. It produces an annual financial statement, which is submitted for verification at a general assembly.

## ARTICLE 9 – LOSS OF MEMBERSHIP

Membership is lost by:
- resignation – which must be addressed to the president of the board and need not be justified;
- removal decided by the board for non-payment of membership fees, non-compliance with the terms of the NumaHOP free license, or for serious misconduct, meaning any action likely to harm, directly or indirectly, the activities of the association or its reputation, after the person concerned has been invited to provide explanations before the board and/or in writing.

## ARTICLE 10 – AFFILIATION

The association may also affiliate with other associations, unions, or groups by decision of the board.

## ARTICLE 11 – GENERAL ASSEMBLY

The assembly includes all members current in their membership fees as of December 31 of the year preceding the date of the general assembly, or for the current year. Each member may be represented by another member of their choice. The number of proxies is limited to two per member present.

Members are convened at least 15 days before the date set for the general assembly, by electronic means. The agenda appears on the notice. Any proposed amendment to the agenda must be submitted at least eight working days before the meeting and will be put to the assembly.

The ordinary general assembly meets each year, in person or by videoconference. Decisions are made by a majority of members present or represented. Decisions of the general assemblies are binding on all members, including those absent or represented.

The president, assisted by board members, chairs the assembly and presents the moral situation of the association. The treasurer reports on management and submits the financial statement for approval. The results of the vote for board elections are communicated at the general assembly. Minutes of the meeting will be prepared.

All deliberations are decided by a show of hands or electronic vote.

Decisions of the general assembly acting in an ordinary capacity are made by an absolute majority of members present or represented.

The general assembly can only validly deliberate if at least one third of its members is present or represented. If the quorum is not reached, a new general assembly may be convened within a minimum of 15 days without requiring a quorum.

## ARTICLE 12 – INTERNAL REGULATIONS

Internal regulations may be established by the board, in which case they are submitted for approval to the general assembly. These regulations are binding on all members of the association and are intended to establish various points not covered by these bylaws, in particular those relating to the internal administration of the association.

## ARTICLE 13 – DISSOLUTION

In the event of dissolution of the association, the general assembly designates one or more liquidators responsible for liquidating the association's assets. The net assets of the association will be allocated in accordance with the law.

_Date of validation of these bylaws: General Assembly of January 22, 2026._
